## Design Context

### Users
This product is used by wholesale traders and higher-level wholesale buyers in a broker-led B2B workflow. Users browse a unified marketplace, shortlist products, and request quotations without seeing the real supplier identity. Their main job is to discover products quickly, judge availability with confidence, and move toward pricing decisions without friction.

### Brand Personality
The brand should feel calm, classic, and luxurious. The emotional goal is confidence through restraint: the interface should feel premium, trustworthy, composed, and commercially serious rather than flashy or playful.

### Aesthetic Direction
The preferred visual direction is luxury with calm clarity. The UI should use refined spacing, quiet hierarchy, elegant typography, and soft premium surfaces. It should support both light mode and dark mode. It should not look like a cold technical interface, a developer tool, or a generic analytics dashboard.

### Design Principles
1. Quiet luxury over visual noise: prioritize elegance, whitespace, and composure instead of heavy decoration.
2. Fast B2B scanning: design for quick recognition of products, filters, status, and actions.
3. Trust through consistency: keep navigation, profile controls, cards, filters, and quotation flows visually aligned across the trader experience.
4. Premium neutrality: use restrained color, strong typography, and subtle emphasis rather than loud accents.
5. Broker-first clarity: the UI should support sourcing, quotation, and marketplace behavior while hiding supplier complexity from the buyer.
